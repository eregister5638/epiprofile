Epipedia of Zinzi Bailey

Epipedia, the free encyclopedia for Epidemiologist Profiles

Zinzi Bailey (born 1985) is a social epidemiologist and an Assistant Professor at the University of Miami, Miller School of Medicine and the Sylvester Comprehensive Cancer Center (UCSF).

Zinzi Bailey
Born: Zinzi Bailey
Education: Princeton University(BA),Emory University(MSPH),Harvard University(ScD)
Title: Assistant Professor at the University of Miami Miller School of Medicine and the Sylvester Comprehensive Cancer Center
Areas of Interest: Cancer Health Disparities, Health Impacts of Policy Solution for Structural and Institutional Discrimination

Zinzi Bailey was born in Jamaica to a RN that worked as a public health nurse. She emigrated to Miami with her family three years later. Some of Bailey’s earliest experiences with public health were when her mother taught her how to wash her hands or recounted her experiences in community home health care. Bailey attributes much of the passion she has for working with marginalized and vulnerable populations to these experiences with her mother. Initially, Bailey wanted to be a physician, but pursued a Bachelor of Arts and Masters Degree in Public Health, after realizing that she wanted to focus on addressing public health systems instead. Bailey’s commitment to addressing structural and institutional discrimination are also founded in the varying degrees of ever-present racism that she faced as a child. She recalls being frustrated by Hurricane Katrina, characterizing it as “a stark, racialized experience that clearly impacted people and communities, resulting in suffering and death. Witnessing it was a seminal moment in my desire to work in public health.”

In 2017, Dr. Bailey joined the Miller School at University of Miami after directing research at New York City Department of Health and Mental Hygiene’s Center for Health Equity. Her current research aims to measure structural racism as it pertains to lung cancer health disparities. Through this research, she looks to create interventions in social and health policy, as well as in neighborhoods. She has also published in the New England Journal and the Lancet, where she has reviewed inequities in the Trump Administration health policies, the effects of discrimination trickling into education and other sectors of life, and how the distribution of resources influence discriminatory thought patterns. Zinzi Bailey also played a focal role in the formation of the Racial Justice Task Force, where she conducts racial-equity research, which is rooted in looking through a lens of structural racism and what outcomes are rooted in this viewpoint. Dr. Bailey’s works place great precedence on the need to make connections and reach out to the community, while dismantling systems of inequality. In order to reach the best possible healthcare, every member of the community must benefit equally and Dr. Bailey’s research has shed light on areas that need improvement. She also emphasizes that being anti-racist is not as simple as being “color-blind”, but instead understanding that certain groups are subject to greater levels of inequity than others.
